    5. You don't need permission if

    • You care for children age 8 and over
    • If you care for children under 8 in domestic premises (eg a home)
    • If you provide a home-education arrangement
    • If you provide extracurricular activities (up to two of the following: study support, sport, performing arts, arts and crafts, religious study)

    You need permission if

    • You want to care for children aged between 5 and 7
    • You are not related to the child
    • You provide care for longer than 2 hours

    You can't have permission if

    • You are under 18
    • You don't have the right to work in the UK
    • You have a criminal record

    Why do I need to be a registered childminder?

    • To ensure you will provide a good standard of care for children

    How long it takes

    Registering usually takes up to:

    • 12 weeks for childminders and home childcarers
    • 25 weeks for a childcare providers on domestic or non-domestic premises
