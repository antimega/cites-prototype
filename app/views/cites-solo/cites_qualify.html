Apply for a CITES permit

CITES is the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora, an international agreement between governments that came into force in 1975. The import, export and use for commercial gain of certain species requires a CITES permit.

CITES species

Over 2,500 animal and 25,000 plant species are included in the Appendices of CITES on Appendix I, II or III. In the European Union the CITES Appendices are replaced by Annexes A, B, C and D. The EU regulations provide a complete list of species controlled by CITES under the EU regulations.

You can find out if a species is controlled under CITES and Appendix and Annex numbers by visiting the species+ website.
